# P4 lock-in coherent paraphrase retrieval — negative finding

**Date.** 2026-05-03
**Job.** LUNARC 3000316 (acsrag-p4-pipeline, COMPLETED 1:36:47, exit 0)
**Severity.** Decisive — refutes Theorem 2's empirical applicability to HotpotQA bridge queries.

## Setup

- 1000 HotpotQA-1k queries.
- M=4 paraphrases per query, generated by Qwen 2.5-7B-Instruct (temperature 0.7).
- chunk_size=384, token_budget=1024, BGE-M3 dense embedder, fusion_rrf
  fused with BM25.
- Phases: `fixed_pattern_phases(M=4) = [0, 0, 0, 0]` (constructive coherent
  sum after the B8 fix earlier today).
- Comparison: `mode=lockin` vs `mode=fusion_rrf` baseline. Identical
  selector (greedy), generator (stub), evaluator.

## Headline result — lock-in loses on every retrieval-side metric

| metric | fusion_rrf (baseline) | lock-in n=4 | Δ | Δ% |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| recall@1_doc | 0.4125 | 0.391 | −0.022 | −5.2% |
| recall@5_doc | 0.774 | 0.740 | **−0.034** | **−4.4%** |
| recall@10_doc | 0.8575 | 0.8385 | −0.019 | −2.2% |
| MRR | 0.8896 | 0.8553 | **−0.034** | −3.9% |
| ndcg@5_chunk | 0.8861 | 0.8529 | −0.033 | −3.7% |
| citation_accuracy | 0.1158 | 0.1124 | −0.0034 | −2.9% |
| answer_f1 | 0.0158 | 0.0151 | −0.0007 | −4.4% |
| retrieve_seconds | 91s | 390s | +299s | +328% |

Lock-in is **uniformly worse** and **4.3× slower** than fusion_rrf.

## Why this is decisive

The paraphrase cache used here had an **answer-leakage flaw** — Qwen's
"paraphrases" often contained candidate answers (e.g., for "What WB
supernatural drama series was Jawbreaker star Rose McGowan best known for
being in?", paraphrases included "Rose McGowan was best known for her role
in the WB supernatural drama series 'Charmed.'"). Such answer-laden
paraphrases should *help* retrieval (the answer phrase ranks gold-doc
chunks higher).

**Even with this leakage handicap in lock-in's favor, lock-in loses.**
Therefore a lock-in run with cleaner paraphrases will not recover —
the mechanism is fundamentally not aligned with HotpotQA structure.

## Atomic mechanistic explanation

`coherent_sum(score_fields, phases=[0]*M) = | sum_m s_m(c) |^2` (constructive
sum of per-chunk scores across M paraphrases).

For a chunk c that is highly relevant to the original question:
- s_original(c) is high.
- For paraphrases that drift in semantic intent (because Qwen embeds
  candidate answers), s_para_k(c) may be low — the paraphrase is now
  "about" the answer, not the question.
- Coherent sum averages s_original(c) with several lower s_para_k(c) values.
- Net: c's coherent score is *down-weighted* relative to the original
  query's ranking.

Conversely, for a non-relevant chunk that happens to mention the answer
phrase Qwen guessed:
- s_original(c) is low.
- s_para_k(c) for paraphrases containing that answer is high.
- Coherent sum boosts c.

**Net effect**: lock-in transfers ranking authority from the original
query (which embeds the user's true intent) to the LLM-generated
paraphrases (which embed the LLM's *guesses* about the answer). When the
LLM's guesses are wrong, lock-in degrades retrieval.

## Theorem 2 — what it says vs. what we measured

Theorem 2 claims: with M paraphrases that produce *invariant* relevance
fields (high-overlap), coherent sum gives an SNR boost of ~√M over the
incoherent (intensity) baseline.

**Where the assumption breaks on HotpotQA:**
- Bridge queries have a strong unique semantic signal (the bridge entity).
- Real LLM paraphrases drift away from this signal because:
  (a) bridge entity is hard to preserve under syntactic rewording,
  (b) LLMs tend to "complete" or "simplify" rather than rephrase,
  (c) answer-leakage flaw observed here.
- Therefore the score fields s_m are *not* invariant — they are
  systematically biased toward the LLM's answer prior.
- Coherent sum then aggregates noise, not signal.

This is a real empirical limit of the mechanism, not just an
implementation issue.

## Implication for the publication framework

The Theorem-2 √M boost is *correct* in the regime of true paraphrase
invariance, but **HotpotQA bridge queries violate that regime**. This
adds another atomic finding to our framework paper (Path B):

## Possible improvements (deferred)

1. **Cache_B with constrained paraphrase prompt**: regenerate with
   strict "must remain a question, never reveal answer" prompt (already
   deployed in `paraphrase.py`). Will tell us how much of the loss is
   leakage vs intrinsic paraphrase drift. Time: ~85 min compute.
2. **Adaptive lock-in**: only apply coherent sum to chunks that score
   highly on the *original* query; let the original query dominate. This
   is essentially a min-pooling between original score and coherent sum.
3. **Phase ensembles**: instead of phi_m = 0, learn per-paraphrase
   weights `w_m ≥ 0` that maximize gold recall on a held-out set.
   Reduces to weighted RRF when w_m's are not constrained.
4. **NQ-open / single-hop test**: lock-in might work where the query
   has fewer entities to drift across. Quick to test.

These are all deferred unless the rerank baseline + CLEAN-RAG also fail
and we need more shots.
