# P2 — Strong baselines + Asimov benchmark + cross-section metric (in progress)

**Goal.** No claim of beating baselines without strong baselines. P2 brings
ColBERT-class late-interaction, RAPTOR-style hierarchical retrieval, and
SPLADE++ learned-sparse into the harness. It also introduces the
**Asimov benchmark** (gold injection at known positions, stage-decomposed
efficiencies) and the **cross-section retrieval metric** (luminosity-
normalized per-method gold-acquisition rate). Together these primitives let
us evaluate methods on a basis the field has not formalized.

## Mechanism statement

A retriever's headline number on a benchmark *confounds* three things: the
intrinsic detection probability per query, the query-mix luminosity, and the
generation efficiency. The cross-section formalism factorizes:

  EM ≈ σ_recall@k · σ_efficiency

with σ defined relative to per-query luminosity L_q = min(|gold_q|, k).
The Asimov benchmark sets ε_chunk = 1 by construction, so the residual gap
between Asimov accuracy and real-benchmark accuracy isolates retriever
imperfection. P2 makes the existing RAG benchmark literature *factorizable*
for the first time in our pipeline.

## Reproduction

Local smoke (no GPU):

```bash
python -m pytest -q          # 36/36 pass

# Asimov benchmark synthesis demo:
cat > /tmp/qa.jsonl <<'EOF'
{"query": "What is the speed of light?", "gold_text": "Light travels at 299792458 m/s in vacuum.", "answer": "299792458"}
EOF
cat > /tmp/distractors.jsonl <<'EOF'
{"text": "Random distractor passage 1."}
{"text": "Random distractor passage 2."}
EOF
rag-build-asimov --qa /tmp/qa.jsonl --distractors /tmp/distractors.jsonl \
  --out /tmp/asimov_demo --pool-size 2 --position middle
```
